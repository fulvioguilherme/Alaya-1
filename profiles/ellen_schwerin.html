About

Serena is a Registered International Board Certified Lactation Consultant in private practice working with mothers in the San Francisco Bay Area in California. She can provide you with the breastfeeding help you need, within the comfort of your own home. She has volunteered for national non-profit breastfeeding organizations,helped to establish a national breastfeeding organization, facilitated breastfeeding support groups for over 7 years and researched breast feeding topics for health care professionals.Serena has authored articles on lactation and breastfeeding for La Leche League International and Native Mothering, an online breastfeeding and mothering resource. Additionally, she presents breastfeeding topics at workshops and conferences. Serena is married and has one child. She is also a newly graduated nurse, and the recipient of the Doctor's Medical Center Academic Achievement Award in Nursing (2014).

Specialities

As part of your health care team, I offer my experience in dealing with common and complex difficulties, by providing in-home supportive care. I assess and make a plan of action for lactation issues that may arise, such as:

  • Thrush
  • breast abscess or complex infections of the breast
  • Low milk supply
  • Over supply
  • Tongue tie
  • Infant feeding difficulty
  • low infant weight gain or FTT
  • IGT , hypoplasia of the breasts
  • breast reduction, breast augmentation and breast surgeries
  • teething in the breastfed baby
  • attachment and bonding
  • oral aversion
  • breastfeeding the late preterm or premature baby
  • multiples
  • painful breastfeeding

Payment & Policies

Payment for Doula Care is due on the 37th week of your pregnancy, for lactation care it is due the end of our visit, I will offer you a receipt for payment with a bill for your insurance company if you would like one. *Not all insurance companies will reimburse for lactation consultant visits but I have had very good success with Google employees and Aetna is currently reimbursing a portion for lactation bills. Currently there is no reimbursement for Doula work. It is your responsibility to check with your insurance carrier about their policy beforehand, and the obligation of payment is yours regardless of insurance or other third party involvement.
